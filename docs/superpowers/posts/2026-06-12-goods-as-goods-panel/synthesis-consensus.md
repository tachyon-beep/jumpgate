# Consensus across all five lenses (each point survived its adversarial critique)

1. **Goods count goes RUNTIME.** All lenses + all critiques converge: replace `enum Resource` (economy.rs:9-24, N_RESOURCES=2) with `Good(pub u16)` + Vec-backed per-good columns, count from a config-tail `GoodsCfg`. The existing folds carry **no count word** (verified: `for r in 0..N_RESOURCES` at hash.rs:399-419; config.rs:607-633), so the representation commit is **provably state-hash-identical** on trophic/frontier — the strongest exit digest of the rung — and Experiment C's 10→20 becomes config-only. Good NAMES never fold (index/count only).

2. **Two format bumps, one per rung.** v6 = the owned `hold` column on ALL craft (rung A; pirate rows hold zeros, the cargo-None precedent stores.rs:179); v7 = JetsamStore + `PirateState.last_fence_station_row` (rung B). Spec §6's singular "first bump since v5" is amended to one-per-rung. The pirate crate hold REUSES the v6 column — no provenance fields stored (deferred means NOT BUILT).

3. **"Byte-identity" exit = behavior digest, never cross-bump hash equality.** HASH_FORMAT_VERSION folds as word 2 of every state hash (hash.rs:148-149), so the spec §6 cross-branch digest must be defined over UNHASHED surfaces: sha256 over stdout ∪ window-JSONL ∪ gossip-log per (scenario, seed) — the WGB phase-1 procedure. State-hash sequence equality is available for exactly one commit: the runtime-goods representation commit (no bump). Instruments land FIRST and pin the digest baseline (the WGB Task-1.2.7 lesson).

4. **Food does not exist.** Spec §3's "keep Ore / Food / Fuel (they carry existing mechanics)" is false — `Resource {Ore, Fuel}` (economy.rs:9-12); "food" is only the pirate ledger `food_micros` (stores.rs:98). Rung A adds **8** goods; Food ships as Good(2) WITH a consumption recipe (input-only `Recipe`, the fuel-sink shape) or WA1 has nothing to read. WA1's "starve" = zero-stock run-length per (station, essential good) + stalled consumer — no station-survival mechanic.

5. **The trade-buy goods leg is a TRANSFER, never the refuel consume.** Refuel books `consumed[Fuel]` because propellant exits the goods ledger (economy.rs:1051-1052); a buy into a hold is stock→hold with NO counter (the try_load shape, economy.rs:826-827). Spec §3's "BuyUpgrade/Refuel-clone settle idiom" is right for intent/dock/money and wrong for the goods leg.

6. **A money counterparty must be named or arbitrage is unrealizable.** Goods move for free in the entire contract path and stations hold no money (StationStore = stock/price only, economy.rs:42-47); corps have NO revenue leg (G1#12) and drain to zero in ~2000 deliveries. The Yard/Port config-corp-index idiom (ShipyardCfg/RefuelCfg corp_index) is the seam; the counterparty must explicitly cover the HAVEN or rung B's fence-resale loop breaks (L3-C4).

7. **Rung A piracy is coherent unchanged, with one named coupling.** Pirates rob contract haulers exactly as today (ransom wallet-clamped at the 6.0M cap, cargo→consumed, food mint, escrow refund); own-cargo traders are **mechanically invisible** — `hauler_is_eligible` requires a live `CargoLoaded|InTransit` contract (verified pirate.rs:375-398). Pre-register: WA3's own-trade share IS the pirate food supply; WA5 is a joint read with WA3 or prey-shrink masquerades as PermanentPeace (`predation_collapsed`, diagnostics.rs:311-321).

8. **D5 re-times pirate food, and the ledger has THREE mint legs.** Rung B removes the rob-time food mint, adds the fence-time mint — and KEEPS the starvation re-grubstake mint (verified `p.food_micros = trophic.grubstake_micros`, pirate.rs:723-725): pirates are structurally immortal welfare recipients; a seizure-without-fence world is a duty-cycle, not extinction. All three legs named; band re-walk at the console accounts for the welfare mint.
